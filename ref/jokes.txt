Knock knock jokes for students (list)
Fun, games, and jokes can be just as valuable a part of the learning experience as serious study and lessons. Adding joy into the mix makes it easier for students to connect with the subject material – and being their favorite teacher certainly doesn’t hurt their enthusiasm. Research has shown that engaging students with humor are an effective and valuable way to break the tension, encourage participation and improve the mood in your classroom.

Whether you’re looking to break the ice with your students at the beginning of the academic year or want to end the day positively, these knock-knock joke ideas are an excellent place to start. Read on for a full selection of classic, modern and educational jokes you might want to try out in your class:

Education resources
Why tell knock-knock jokes?
You may be wondering, why tell knock-knock jokes at all? There are a few reasons. Aside from being a generally harmless and enjoyable joke that students of all ages can enjoy, joking in the classroom has several benefits. Firstly, building a rapport with students is vital to improving teaching and effectively engaging students in their work. Being enthusiastic, smiling, making eye contact, and generally lightening the mood in your classroom are all extremely helpful for building positive relationships with your students.

By becoming a positive force for your students, you can hold their attention and encourage them to enjoy learning. Jokes are an excellent, low-pressure way to get students chatting and laughing, particularly if you extend your curriculum to allow students to make and tell their jokes. Another way joking is beneficial is that it helps students build positive relationships among themselves. By encouraging joke-telling and communication, you can help children build friendships in the classroom. Whether you provide a list of knock-knock jokes or encourage students to make their own, a happy classroom is a more positive and engaged classroom overall.
